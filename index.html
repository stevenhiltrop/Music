There are 2 half tones in music. B > C and E > F. This is very important to know as when we are calculating our chords through progressions, modes or tones. We might have a sharp or flat. More of this further down. Sharps are indicated by # and are a half tone above/up. Flats are indicated by b and are a half tone below/under.

Note

Commonly musicians joke with other musicians about a B# or Cb being off. This is a teaser to understand at what level they are at. Commonly used with people who just joined a band. Never reply with yea I can play that in a serious tone.

Intervals

One of the most important things to know in music composition but weridly enough, not understood by many. First we determine the root note. This can be for the measure, the whole bar or the whole song. Let's take C the root. The 2nd> interval would be D. The 3rd E and the 7th B. Intervals are usually indicated with Roman numbers. The sequence is I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII respectivly.

Triads

Triads are the most common and easiest way to create music. Often used in the Pop and Rock genre for music composition.
On the guitar this can be translated to accoustic chords or power chords without using your pinky.
The triad exists of the root tone, the 3rd and the 5th note.

Chord progressions

Ever wondered why so many songs sound good? Ever wondered why they all sound the same? Do you have a preferecd music genre?
A chord progression is what music is all about. You take 1 chord you like and make up a random progression. That's it!
To appriciate music is to have a good sense of hearing, recognise the rhytm and chord progressions and the changes during. Most rich musicians only make songs of 4 chords per song. This makes it simple enough for the common person to appriciate. Cause they can snap their fingers to it and recognise the pattern.

Scales

Blues scale (major)

I - IIIb - IV - Vb - V - VIIb

A typical major blues scale exists of 12 measures.

I7 - I7 - I7 - I7

IV7 - IV7 - I7 -I 7

V7 - IV7 - I7 - I7

Examples

I IIIb IV Vb V VIIb
Bb Db Eb E F Ab

Note

For a major blues scale you can replace the last 2 notes of the last measure with V7 as this another dominant tone.
Measure 3 would look like this: V7 - IV7 - V7 - V7

Scales are what makes a lick. These are continiously stolen from artists and adopted in solos.
This is perfectly fine to do. If you plan to make money out of music, then make sure you do not have 8 or more notes that are used in another song for legal reasons.

Tetrachords

The idea behind tetrachords is fairly straight forward:

  • Add 1 full note
  • Add 1 full note
  • Add 1 half note
  • Repeat once more
Each tetra chords consists of 2 lines containing 4 notes. This becomes clear with the example below.
Keep in mind that B-C and E-F are half tones, automaticly adding a sharp or flat should a step be a full note on these.
